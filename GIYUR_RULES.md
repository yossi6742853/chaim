# Giyur Rules — Adapting Games for the Haredi Community

## Purpose
This document defines how to "convert" (מגייר) a general/secular game into one suitable for the Haredi (Ultra-Orthodox Jewish) community, matching Chaim Katz's educational approach.

---

## Content Rules

### 1. Music & Entertainment
- **Remove** all references to songs by male or female singers/artists
- **Remove** any requirement to play recorded music (Spotify, YouTube, etc.)
- **Allowed:** clapping, humming, singing group songs (zemiros), drumming, rhythm-making with body percussion
- **Allowed:** using a simple timer buzzer or whistle

### 2. Gambling & Money
- **No** prizes involving real money or anything resembling gambling
- **No** betting mechanics, even symbolic
- **Allowed:** symbolic prizes (candy, stickers, small treats), team points, honor-based rewards

### 3. Gender Separation
- All games must be designed for **single-gender groups** (boys OR girls, never mixed)
- **Remove** any pair activities that assume mixed-gender pairing
- If the original game requires couples, adapt to "pairs" or "partners" (שותפים)
- **Never** reference dating, romantic relationships, or attraction

### 4. Language & Modesty (Tznius)
- Use **modest, respectful language** throughout
- **No** slang, crude humor, or body-related jokes
- **No** physical contact between participants beyond what's necessary for the game mechanics
- For trust falls and similar: ensure appropriate physical boundaries
- **Replace** "hug" with "handshake" or "high-five" (or remove)
- Game descriptions should be warm but dignified

### 5. Cultural References
- **Remove** all references to modern Western pop culture (movies, TV shows, celebrities, social media influencers)
- **Remove** references to non-Jewish holidays (Christmas, Halloween, etc.)
- **Replace** with Jewish/Torah references where appropriate:
  - Characters → Torah personalities, Jewish historical figures
  - Holidays → Jewish holidays (Shabbat, Sukkot, Purim, Pesach)
  - Themes → Jewish values (chesed, emunah, achdut, kavod)
- **Allowed:** general knowledge, nature, science, geography, history

### 6. Materials & Props
- **No** empty alcohol bottles, wine bottles, or beer cans (even as props)
- **No** playing cards with standard face cards (Kings, Queens, Jacks) — use numbered cards or custom cards
- **No** images of immodestly dressed people
- **Allowed:** balls, ropes, blindfolds, cones, paper, markers, natural materials, food items (kosher)

### 7. Values Integration
- Every adapted game should include a **debrief/reflection** component connecting to Jewish values:
  - Teamwork → Achdut (אחדות, unity)
  - Trust → Emunah (אמונה, faith) and Bitachon (בטחון, trust in Hashem)
  - Leadership → Manhigut (מנהיגות) as in Moshe Rabbeinu
  - Helping others → Chesed (חסד, kindness)
  - Perseverance → Hishtadlut (השתדלות, effort)
  - Communication → Shalom (שלום, peace) and Lashon Tov (לשון טוב, positive speech)
- Add 2-3 debrief questions that connect the game experience to real life and Torah values

### 8. Shabbat Awareness
- Note if a game can be played on Shabbat (no writing, no tearing, no electricity)
- Mark games as "Shabbat-friendly" (שבת-friendly) when applicable

---

## Structural Adaptation

### Game Flow (matching Chaim's style)
1. **Opening** (פתיחה): Brief energizer or warm-up — 2-3 minutes
2. **Explanation** (הסבר): Clear, step-by-step rules in simple Hebrew
3. **Play** (משחק): The main activity — with built-in escalation/challenge levels
4. **Reflection** (רפלקציה): Group discussion connecting the experience to values — 3-5 minutes

### Naming Convention
- Give the game a **Hebrew name** that sounds natural and inviting
- Avoid transliteration of English names when a good Hebrew alternative exists
- Names should hint at the game's essence or excitement

### Difficulty & Engagement
- Add variation levels (easy/medium/hard) when possible
- Include a "challenge upgrade" option for groups that finish quickly
- Ensure games work for the specified age range without feeling childish or too complex

---

## Quality Checklist

Before finalizing an adapted game, verify:
- [ ] No music by artists/singers required
- [ ] No gambling or money prizes
- [ ] Designed for single-gender groups
- [ ] Modest language throughout
- [ ] No pop culture or non-Jewish cultural references
- [ ] All materials are kosher/appropriate
- [ ] Debrief questions connect to Jewish values
- [ ] Hebrew name is natural and appealing
- [ ] Rules are clear and complete
- [ ] Age range and group size are realistic
